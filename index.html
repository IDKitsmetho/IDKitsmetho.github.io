Travel to Alaska

Why Should I Travel to Alaska?

Alaska has many great features, such as;

  • Amazing views
  • Many glaciers
  • Many Rivers and Lakes
  • Not very crowded
  • An unbothered natural beauty of nature

Great Tourist Spots in Alaska

  1. The Northern Lights
  2. Denali National Park
  3. Talkeetna City
  4. Alyeska Ski Resort
  5. Chilkoot Trail
  6. Iliamna Lake
  7. Tracy Arm Fjord
  8. University of Alaska Museum of the North

Fun-facts About Alaska

  • There is over 3 million lakes
  • More volcanoes than any other state
  • Home to 17 of North America's tallest mountains
  • The state flower is the, "forget-me-not"
  • Has a nickname of The Last Frontier
  • Glaciers cover 5% of the state
  • 49th State of America

What is Alaska Like in General

In Alaska there is great views everywhere throughout the year. It has
nice fishing along with great hunting too! There are no state taxes, and
real estate is not so expensive. The temperature gets low in the long
winter with lots of snow, and not too much sun. However in the summer,
the temperature can rise up to an average in the 80's and 90's. You can
get very good at snow sports, and you can spend a lot of time outdoors.
It is a layed back state, so life seems to move a bit slower. The flag is
dark blue with eight gold stars forming the Big Dipper and the North
Star.

Alaska's History

It was sold to America by Russia for 7.2 million dollars. Gold was found
so it became a place were gold-seekers would come. In 1900 the Capitol
moved from Sitka to Juneau. In WW2 Alaska lost two of its islands and
had to wait a year before the military would get them back. It became a
state almost 100 years after buying it,the 49th state in 1949. Oil was
discovered but over 100 million acres of land was put aside for
preservation and protection throughout Alaska in 1980.
